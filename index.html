* the long version

I'm Kanishk — a developer who got into security because breaking things taught me more about building them than any tutorial ever did. Most days I'm bouncing between writing code, reading writeups, training models on questionable datasets, and explaining to people that no, I'm not going to "hack their ex's Instagram."

I started out doing CTFs as a way to procrastinate from coursework, which then became coursework, which then became a hobby that pays. Now I help organize them too — turns out designing puzzles is just as fun as solving them, and only mildly more frustrating.

* things I care about

  • Software that respects the user's time, attention, and intelligence.
  • Documentation that doesn't assume you've already read the source.
  • Brutalist design — function with personality, not function pretending to be art.
  • Open source, but the kind people actually maintain.
  • Teaching what I learn, badly at first, better with practice.
